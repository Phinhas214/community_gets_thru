Community Gets Thru

Project Thesis

"Community Gets Thru" is a project created to foster and build community. To many, this point in time feels like an apocalypse! So many horrible things are happening and it is hard to cope with the immensity of it all. I created "Community Gets Thru" as a way to remind people that throughout history when times are thoughest, communities have always pulled through and got stuff done! This is a reminder that all of our POC and LGBTQ communities have worked hard to get us where we are and we can continue by sharing community! This project's goal is to foster healing and understanding and not debate or retraumatize. I am choosing not to specify what atrocities are going on because stepping back and focusing on yourself and your community can help so you're ready for the next fight ahead. Rest is Radical. Take your time.

What is Community?

Community is a group of people that share a certain characteristic. What are some examples? Your school or work is a community. The people you practice your spiritual or religious beliefs with are your community. Even your age group can be considered a community. You can also be part of multiple communities at the same time! The term of community is more broad than most would expect. But to have a deep connection with the people in your communities, everyone has to put in some effort and care.

Participate

If you want to try this activity yourself, do it! Print out the post card design and have your freinds, family, and other community members color and decorate. During or after decorating, start a conversation about community.

What if you don't feel like you have a community to do this activity with? That's ok! Sometimes it's hard to find like minded people. But community doesnt have to be people just like you. It can be people you regularly gather with. There may be people around for you that don't come to mind quickly. What if I'm in a community that doesn't feel like it supports me? This happens a lot. Perhaps refelecting on these questions can help you find a people you share more with or who care about you needs and interests despite differences.

These conversations can feel trivial or basic, but when you meditate and really sit on these questions, you might realize something you don't expect. You might hear a perspective you've never heard. Or you can use these questions and your answers as afirmations on what to focus on in coming times.

Questions

Here are some starter questions for your group activity. They are arranged by how you are addressing them with a group or by yourself. Not all questions are suited for all communities.

Light Group Questions

    How would you describe your community?
    Who is in your community?
    What qualities does your community have that makes you happy, safe, valued, proud?
    What types of ativities do you do in your community?
    What would you love to do in your community that hasn't been done?
    What are some traditions of your community?
    What is a new tradition your community could start?
    What is something your community does that you are looking forward to?

Deeper Group Questions

    What is unique about your closest friends in your community?
    What makes the people in your community different from each other?
    What beliefs do your community share and not share?
    What qualities does your community have that makes you worried, sad, unheard, unsafe?
    What are some things you and your community can do to make you feel happy, safe, valued, proud?

Self Reflection Questions

    What qualities does your community have that makes you worried, sad, unheard, unsafe?
    What can you ask of others to help make you feel happy, safe, valued, proud?
    Where do I see myself in relation to my community in the next year, 3 years, 5 years?
    Does this community let and help me grow?
    Is this community supporting me? Or should I invest efforts in another community?
    What issues or struggles can I come to my community for help with?
    What issues or struggles can I not come to my community for help with?
